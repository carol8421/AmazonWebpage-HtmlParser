4.0 out of 5 stars Replace juice boxes with these., November 19, 2013
By 
Crafty mama (Wynnewood, PA) - See all my reviews
This review is from: Ball 8-Ounce Quilted Crystal Jelly Jars with Lids and Bands, Set of 12 (Kitchen)
After watching one too many videos of funky juice boxes, we decided to look for something that would fit in my son's lunchbox to hold juice. All of the plastic cups were impossible to clean, and had straws that grew black mold because I wasn't scrubbing them with pipe cleaners.
So out of frustration, I grabbed a jelly jar, and put some Spiderman stickers on it to make it cool. I was afraid it would leak, so I wrapped it in a cloth napkin, then put it upright in my kindergarten son's lunchbox.
B000IG2H4S

, right next to his thermos.
B0025Y6742

It's been a month, and we haven't had a leak yet, perhaps because the bag holds the cup upright in transit. All of the other kids think his cup is cool, so I just ordered another case so I can have a cup for my toddler as well when we go to the park or zoo. My toddler tends to take a few sips, and I was left carrying around a half full juicebox - this should do the trick.

Oh, and after a month of use, I'm not worried about this cracking and breaking. I guess since its made to withstand the pressure of canning, it's made out of very strong glass. For about a buck per cup, this is one of the best mom hacks I've found so far.
