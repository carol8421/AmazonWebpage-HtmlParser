5.0 out of 5 stars I get 1 box of 3 refills every other month and it's perfect to keep the diaper odors completely contained, February 24, 2015
By 
Maggie M - See all my reviews
Verified Purchase(What's this?)
This review is from: Playtex Diaper Genie Refill, 270 count (pack of 3) (Health and Beauty)
Get a Diaper Genie and then subscribe to these. I get 1 box of 3 refills every other month and it's perfect to keep the diaper odors completely contained. This is also a savings over buying them individually.

This is basically a long tube of plastic. You pull it out of the top and tie a knot, pushing that into your Diaper Genie. When the canister is full, you use the internal "cutter" to cut the plastic at that point and tie a knot in the top. Voila! Completely contained diapers ready to go out with your trash! Then you tie another knot in the plastic that remains in the Genie to create a new empty trash bag, ready to take more diapers. It's much easier to do than explain.

When you first open a refill, it's a little hard to find the end of the plastic bag and pull it out, but you'll get it.

Some of my family members will twist the refill to "seal" the bag over dirty diaper and that is NOT necessary. When we go to empty the Diaper Genie, we end up with diapers individually wrapped like sausages and then it unwraps to reveal that we've wasted a lot of the plastic bag refill. The Diaper Genie itself will cover the smell. If it's that bad, just cut the bag and tie it.

3.0 out of 5 stars There is a better, less expensive alternative., November 29, 2014
By 
Amazon Customer - See all my reviews
Verified Purchase(What's this?)
This review is from: Playtex Diaper Genie Refill, 270 count (pack of 3) (Health and Beauty)
Playtex Diaper Genie refills are probably more suitable to babies who are not yet consuming baby or table food. As we all know, a baby's 'aroma therapy' (lol) becomes a lot stronger as they progress to solid foods, and the Diaper Genie did a substandard job at containing the odor after my child turned six months. Another Amazon customer recommended using Glad Odor Shield bags in lieu of the refills, and it works PERFECTLY (and is a more cost-effective alternative). Save the plastic ring that comes with the refills, and wrap the top of the plastic bag around the ring (Start with the bag inside of the ring and wrap it around the perimeter). Then, pull the draw string and tie it in a bow underneath the refill ring. Next, push the bottom of the bag through the opening of the Diaper Genie (the same opening you use to place soiled diapers inside of the genie). Finally, place the ring and plastic bag in the Diaper Genie, and it is ready for use. My baby is a little stinker :-), and I can now open the Diaper Genie without being hit by a wave of funk in my face. :-) I paid approximately $11 for a box of 80 Glad Odor Shield kitchen bags on Amazon, so this method is also saving me a lot of money.
